06Product Requirements Document (PRD)

Internal Document v1.2

B2B Poultry Procurement Platform

Status: APPROVED

1. Overview & Objectives

The B2B Poultry Procurement Platform connects primary poultry farmers directly with institutional buyers (restaurants, cloud kitchens), eliminating 2-3 layers of middlemen. This guarantees a higher net margin for farmers while securing consistent pricing and supply for buyers.

  • Goal 1: Onboard 500 verified farmers within Q1.
  • Goal 2: Achieve a daily transaction volume of 5 tons.
  • Goal 3: Maintain a < 5% order failure/rejection rate.

2. Target Audience & Personas

Persona A: The Primary Producer

  • Needs quick liquidity (max D+1 settlement).
  • Low tech literacy; prefers voice/WhatsApp over complex UI.
  • Highly sensitive to daily market price fluctuations.

Persona B: The Institutional Buyer

  • Needs predictable supply for daily operations.
  • Requires standard quality grading (weight, cut type).
  • Prefers weekly/monthly invoicing over daily payments.

3. Core Functional Requirements

REQ-01: Dynamic Pricing Engine

System must ingest daily Mandi (wholesale market) rates and automatically calculate the platform buy/sell spread based on a dynamic margin logic (targeting 10-15%). Must push SMS alerts to supply side.

REQ-02: Escrow-lite Payment Flow

Buyer funds must be verified/held before dispatch. Farmer payout must be triggered automatically via API upon digital proof of delivery (POD) from the logistics partner.

REQ-03: Quality Assurance Matrix

App must require photos of the batch at loading. Implement a standardized dispute resolution flow if the buyer rejects the batch upon delivery.

08Retrospective & Learnings

The Hardest Pivot

Initial MVP focused too much on data feeds. Pivoting to direct transactional capabilities proved to be the core value driver for adoption. We quickly realized farmers needed cash flow more than they needed dashboards.

First Principles

To mitigate operational risks, we partnered with local logistics aggregators rather than owning fleet assets. Trust was built via escrow-like payment structures, ensuring operational transparency and zero asset liability.
